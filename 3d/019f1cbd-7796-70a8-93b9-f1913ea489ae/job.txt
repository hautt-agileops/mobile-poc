3D Artist Needed — Convert Statue Products into Shopify AR (USDZ + GLB)
Posted yesterday
Worldwide

Summary
We sell lifesize, limited-edition collector statues (luxury sculpture e-commerce brand) and want to add "View in AR" functionality to our Shopify product pages so customers can see the true-to-scale statue in their own room before buying.

We need a 3D artist to:

Build (or optimize an existing source file into) an accurate, photorealistic 3D model of our statues — a lifesize Iron Man Mark VII sculpture. for example

Deliver it in both USDZ (iOS AR Quick Look) and GLB (Android Scene Viewer / Shopify native 3D) formats.

Model must be built or scaled to true real-world dimensions: 82.65" H x 82.5" W x 44.5" D. This is non-negotiable — the entire point of the AR feature is for customers to see accurate scale in their space.

Optimize poly count / texture size for fast mobile loading without losing visual fidelity (this will sit on a product page people view on phones).

Upload and configure the files directly in our Shopify product media (we'll provide store access), confirm AR launches correctly from both iOS Safari (Quick Look) and Android Chrome (Scene Viewer).

Test on at least one real iOS and one real Android device before calling it done — not just simulator/preview.

We may have source reference material (renders, photos, possibly a manufacturer CAD file we're tracking down) to speed up modeling — will share in interview.

This could lead to ongoing work: we have ~150 products across 8 collections and plan to roll AR out across the catalog if this first one goes well.

Requirements

Portfolio showing prior product-to-AR conversions, ideally Shopify specifically
Experience with USDZ and GLB export pipelines (Blender, Cinema 4D, or similar)
Understanding of real-world scale accuracy in AR (not just visually plausible — actually correct dimensions)
Comfortable working in Shopify admin (3D model upload under product media)
Can test on real iOS + Android hardware, not just emulators

Nice to Have

Experience with photogrammetry (in case we end up needing to scan a physical unit rather than model from renders)
Prior work in collectibles, toys, or luxury goods e-commerce
